About Me

I am a passionate Biomedical Engineer with a Master's degree from George Washington University, specializing in medical device development, microfluidic systems, and point-of-care diagnostics. My journey in biomedical engineering combines technical expertise with a deep commitment to improving healthcare accessibility.

With hands-on experience in CAD design, prototyping, and experimental validation, I thrive on transforming complex medical challenges into innovative, practical solutions. My work on the Advanced Microfluidic Engineered Screw (AMES) project has allowed me to push the boundaries of diagnostic technology, creating devices that are both highly sensitive and accessible.

When I'm not designing medical devices, you can find me exploring emerging technologies, contributing to open-source projects, or mentoring aspiring engineers.

I'm always excited to connect with professionals who share my passion for healthcare innovation and medical technology advancement.
